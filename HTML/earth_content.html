Current situation

  • 400 million tons of hazardous waste is produced annually around the globe. The rubbish we globally generate is enough to fill 63 000 waste removal trucks. Much of this refuse causes land pollution after being disposed.
  • 6 million hectares were destroyed by soil erosion in 2011.
  • The planet loses 24 billion tonnes of topsoil each year because of land pollution.
  • 80% of the world’s forest has been destroyed already. In South Africa 90% of the tropical forest cover is demolished.

Causes

  • Waste materials generated from human activities
  • Bad agricultural practices such as overusing pesticides and fertilizers as well as burning forests to make space for farmland
  • Deforestation
  • Acid rain

Effects

  • By far the most impactful effect is reduction in crop yields; crops can also become polluted, poisoning humans and animals that consume them
  • Leads to other types of pollution, most notably water pollution
  • Destroys the ecosystem
  • Causes cancers, skin diseases, respiratory disorders, and birth defects for pregnant women.

Solutions

  • “The 3 Rs”: Reduce Waste, Reuse things and Recycle things
  • Buy biodegradable products
  • Dispose of garbage properly
