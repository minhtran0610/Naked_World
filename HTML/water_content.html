Current situation

  • Every day, 2 million tons of sewage and industrial and agricultural waste are discharged into the world’s water the equivalent of the weight of the entire human population of 6.8 billion people.
  • The UN estimates that the amount of wastewater produced annually is about 1,500 km3, six times more water than all the rivers of the world.
  • Lack of adequate sanitation contaminates water courses worldwide and is one of the most significant forms of water pollution. Worldwide, 2.5 billion people live without improved sanitation. Over 70% of these people who lack sanitation, or 1.8 billion people, live in Asia.
  • Water pollution caused 1.8 million deaths in 2015, according to a study published in The Lancet
  • Meanwhile, only 1% of the world's water is freshwater available for using

Causes

  • Human dumping of untreated wastes and sewage into water bodies
  • Oil spills
  • Residue from chemicals used in agriculture such as pesticides or fertilizers

Effects

  • Poisons animals, especially aquatic species, causing damage to the ecosystem
  • Causes multiple diseases in humans, ranging from respiratory infections and skin irritation to hormone disruption, altered brain functions and cancer
    • >

Solutions

  • Dispose of garbage properly; do not litter
  • Do not waste water. Take showers instead of baths and do not keep water taps running
  • Be mindful of using chemicals like pesticides or fertilizers
