Current situation

  • The most light-polluted spot in the world is Moscow, Russia, which is on average 8.1 times brighter than the global urban average
  • In a 2010 article from the Ecology and Society Journal, Hölker and others stated the use of artificial lighting increases by 20% each year, depending on the region
  • Unshielded light fixtures emit more than 50% of their light skyward or sideways. In many instances, only 40% of the light emitted actually illuminates the ground. Nearly 30% of outdoor lighting is wasted due to this poor design. In the United States alone, wasted lighting accounts for 1.7 million tons of carbon dioxide and $2.2 billion in wasted electricity each year.

Causes

  • Excessive, misdirected or invasive use of artificial outdoor lighting.
  • Poorly designed residential, commercial, and industrial outdoor lights.

Effects

  • Contributes to other forms of pollution, most notably air pollution, by releasing harmful chemicals into the air while suppressing natural air cleansing process.
  • Wastes massive amounts of energy, further damaging the environment
  • Disrupts circadian rhythms (the physical, mental and behavioral changes that occur in a 24-hour cycle. The circadian clock regulates physiologic activities such as brain wave patterns, hormone production, and cell regulation), leading to various health issues

Solutions

  • Turn off lamps while not using
  • Switch to efficient types of lightbulbs
  • Place limits on light levels allowed on advertisement boards and store fronts
