Current situation

  • 2 million deaths every year as a result of exposure to ambient (outdoor) air pollution
  • 8 million deaths every year as a result of household exposure to smoke from dirty cookstoves and fuels
  • 91% of the world’s population lives in places where air quality exceeds WHO guideline limits

Causes

  • Solid and liquid particles and certain gases that are suspended in the air pollutes it.
  • The solid and liquid particles suspended in our air are called aerosols. Aerosols can come from human activities that involve burning fossil fuels such as coal or gas. Cars, factories and gas or coal cooking stoves are example of this category. Natural sources including dust, pollen, mold spores, volcanoes and wildfires also produce aerosols.
  • The presence of certain gases in the atmosphere, as well as the lack of others can also lead to air pollution. Some examples of harmful gases are carbon monoxide (CO), carbon dioxide (CO2)

Health effects

  • Air pollution can lead to multiple respiratory diseases, such as asthma, pneumonia and other heart and lung diseases. Those who are already suffering from illnesses such as asthma, as well as children and the elderly, are particularly vulnerable.
  • Air pollution has also been linked to fatigue, headaches and anxiety; irritation of the eyes, nose and throat; damage to reproductive organs; harm to the liver, spleen and blood, as well as nervous system damage.
Pollutant Health effects at very high levels
Nitrogen Dioxide, Sulphur Dioxide, Ozone These gases irritate the airways of the lungs, increasing the symptoms of those suffering from lung diseases
Particles: dust, mold, etc. Fine particles can be carried deep into the lungs where they can cause inflammation and a worsening of heart and lung diseases
Carbon Monoxide This gas prevents the uptake of oxygen by the blood. This can lead to a significant reduction in the supply of oxygen to the heart, particularly in people suffering from heart disease

Solutions

  • Using public transport
  • Turning off electronic devices when they are not being used
  • Understanding and applying the concept of Reduce, Reuse, Recycle
  • Emphasizing on clean sources of energy such as solar, wind or hydro power
  • Switching to more efficient devices
